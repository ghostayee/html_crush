Name: Kennedy Johnson

About Me

I’m Kennedy Johnson, a chemist based in Kenya with a deep passion for turning waste into energy and ideas into reality. I specialize in designing and scaling small-to-medium biofuel systems biodiesel, bioethanol, and biogas using agricultural residues, waste oils, and whatever biomass is locally available. My focus is on low-cost reactors, reusable catalysts, zero-waste processes, and solutions that actually work for rural and off-grid communities. In the lab I live with GC-MS, FTIR, titration rigs, technical writing, and patent-ready documentation. When I’m not in the lab, you’ll find me solo backpacking or overlanding across East Africa’s wildest routes, testing my fuels in real world conditions and building a biofuel-powered expedition rig from the ground up. I document everything—science, failures, sunrises, and breakdowns because sustainable living only counts when it survives the dirt roads. I also code (React.js, Node.js) and build my own sites when the signal finally appears in the middle of nowhere. I’m always open to collaborations on green energy projects, rural biofuel implementation, adventure-science storytelling, technical consulting, or anything that combines rigorous chemistry with a proper view. If you have waste you want to turn into fuel, a remote community that needs clean energy, or an expedition that could use a chemist who doesn’t mind no roads and no Wi-Fi, let’s talk.
